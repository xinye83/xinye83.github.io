Summary

I was an undergraduate student at Wuhan University. It is widely considered one of the most beautiful campuses in China, the photo on this page gives you a glimpse of the main campus during fall. My major was Mathematical Economics and Finance, I graduated with Bachelor's degrees in both Economics and Applied Mathematics.

During the summer of 2013, I traveled to the US and started my graduate study in the Department of Mathematics at Purdue University. My research interests lie in numerical linear algebra, matrix computation and high performance computing, my research works were mainly in the field of hierarchical matrix algorithms, numerical eigenvalue problems and numerical analysis. I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Computational Mathematics in 2018 under the supervision of Jianlin Xia.

After that, I worked as a Postdoctoral Researcher under the supervision of Yousef Saad at University of Minnesota, Twin City for a year. My projects were about numerical eigenvalue problems and preconditioning techniques for large sparse matrices.

You can check my academic CV here.

Resource

Some resources I find useful for my own study and research.

  • Matrix collections, good places to find test matrices for your course works and research projects.
    • Matrix Market.
    • SuiteSparse Matrix Collection, formerly the University of Florida Sparse Matrix Collection, maintained by Tim Davis.
    • Structured Matrix Market, a collection of structured and hierarchical matrices maintained by Jianlin Xia.
  • Mailing lists.
    • NA-Digest, free to subscribe.
    • SIAM-CSE, free if you have active membership in SIAG.
  • Check Detexify if you forget the Latex command for some math symbol.
  • An Abstract Algebra note I compiled when I was learning Latex and preparing for my qualifying exam, it has all definitions, propositions, theorems and corollaries from Chapter 1 through 14 of Abstract Algebra, Third Edition by Dummit and Foote.
  • French Glossary for reading mathematical texts by Kai-Wen Lan.
